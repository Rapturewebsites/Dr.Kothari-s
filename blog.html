What is Homeopathy and Homeopathic Medicine?

A wide range of ailments can be treated with homeopathy, a natural medical method. It is predicated on the Law of Similars and the infinitesimal dose, which are its two main tenets. Law of Similars : In contrast to conventional medicine, homeopathy views sickness symptoms as the body's attempts to heal itself. To support the body's natural healing processes, homeopaths administer remedies that would elicit the same symptoms in a healthy person rather than suppressing symptoms.

Infinitesimal Dose: Homeopathy is also recognized as a pharmaceutical science. To achieve a dose of up to one part in a million, the compounds are carefully diluted in alcohol or water using ingredients derived from plants, minerals, animals, or chemicals. Small sugar globules that have been diluted in liquid are given to the patient. Homeopathy treats the whole individual rather than just the ailment they are now experiencing. This implies that two patients with the same ailment may have two different treatments and solutions. This is due to the fact that each individual is viewed as a complete rather than as a machine made up of several pieces.

Dr. Samuel Hahnemann 1755-1843

About Dr. Samuel Hahnemann, M.D.

Samuel Christian Frederic Hahnemann was born in 1755, at Meissen, Saxony in Germany. He established the fundamental principles of the science he named 'Homeopathy', that is derived from the Greek terms signifying similarity and suffering, meaning “homeo” and “pathos'' respectively. He is also considered the “Father of Experimental Pharmacology” because he was the first physician to prepare medicines in a specialized way by proving them on healthy human beings to determine how the medicines acted to cure diseases. Before Hahnemann, medicines were given on speculative indications, mainly on the basis of authority without experimental verification. Dr. Hahnemann embraced the law of cure known as "Similia Similibus Curentur", or "Like Cures Like". He was the progenitor of several modern medical approaches, and he treated thousands of difficult and chronic cases from all over Europe that other doctors could not treat. Thus, he became so famous that physicians from Europe and America came to him to learn the science of Homeopathy.
